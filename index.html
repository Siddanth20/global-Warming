INTRODUCTION

What is Global Warming?

The increase in average temperature of earth due to the presence of Green House Gases

Green House Gases

They are the gases which contribute in retaining heat in the athmosphere.
They are:
Carbon Dioxide, Methane, Watervapour, Oxides of Nitrogen, Chlorofluorocarbons

Green House Effect

Warming up of earth's surface, due to concerntration or blanketting effect of the greenhouse gases.

IMPACT

Rainfall

There is uncontrallable rainfall. Day by day floods are increasing throughout the globe.

Seasons

There is lot of shifting of seasons which is happening. Seasons are becoming unpredictable. Winters are becoming short whereas the summers are becoming longer and hotter each year.

Ecosystem

Causes large scale of destruction of ecosytem and extinction of animals.

Agriculture and Plant Life

The soil fertility and amount of soil water retained is slowly decreasing.

PROTECTION

How to protect the earth from Global Warming

Technological Measures


We can use alternate sources of energies such as solar energy, wind energy, Biogas, Biofuels, Hydro Power, Hydrogen Energy which inturn emits less carbon dioxide.

Factories can filter out the carbon dioxide before leaving it to air.

Policies


Government should work towards less burning of fossils fuels such as wood and coal.

Fuels such as L.P.G, L.N.G and C.N.G can be used.

Taxes can be put for Industries emitting Carbon-Dioxide in large amounts.

Social Measures


Awareness programs and campaigns can be set up in different regions which give information on global warming.

We can reduce electricity consumption.
